introduction

Coachella Valley is the intersection of Colorado River Project and California State Water Project. Large scale water infrastructures there are so prominent that it juxtaposes with the desert landscape, with snow mountain landscape. However, even the aqueduct construction solved the current problem, conducting water is not a sustainable method. Then what is the future for Coachella Valley?

The ruins of old infrastructure provides an opportunity for a beautiful afterlife. Architecture structures can be proposed, engaging the existing building, for people to explore and appreciate the ruins.



research | mapping

With the help of ArcGIS(Geographic Information System) and the group site visit to Coachella Valley, we gathered information and mapped out various water facilities and infrastructures in the larger Coachella area, including reclamation plants, ground water replenishment facilities, water treatment plants and etc.

(group exercise, credit: Armaan Shah, Rory Thibault, Paul Wu, Josh Stevens)

With the first path exploration of levels and forms, I constructed a sectional model to combine the levels and integrate with the existing water facility. The meandering staircases, like a labyrinth lead people to explore the space from various angles and through different routes.
